The PI3K/AKT and Wnt signaling pathways are central regulators of cell survival, proliferation, and differentiation, and are frequently dysregulated in cancer. In the immune system, both pathways play critical roles in immune cell development and function. Emerging evidence suggests that these pathways do not act in isolation but instead engage in complex crosstalk to coordinate cellular outcomes. However, the molecular mechanisms underlying this interaction, particularly in the context of immune cell development and hematologic malignancies, remain poorly understood. This work aims to elucidate how PI3K/AKT and Wnt signaling pathways interact to regulate immune cell fate decisions and function, and how their dysregulation contributes to the development of blood cancers.
